Feeds

Elements or ingredients required for feeding include: carbohydrates, proteins, vitamins, minerals and non-nutrients. Carbohydrates should be about 75%, proteins 15%, vitamins 5%, minerals 3% and non-nutrients 2%. Examples of carbohydrates include pollard(32/= per kg), maize bran (20/= per kg) and maize jam (28/= per kg). Proteins include soya meal(72/= per kg), cotton seed cake(45/= per kg) and sunflower seed cakes at (25/= per kg). Vitamins include: vitamin supplement and fresh cowdung. Minerals include: DCP(25/= per kg), lime (20/= per kg) and greer(28/= per kg) Well, as for feeds with regards to age, chickmash is given from (1 to 8 weeks), growers mash from (8-18 weeks) and layers mash from (18 to 24 weeks)

Vaccinations

Newcastle disease is the most common type of poultry diseases. Symptoms are foamy eyes and nose discharge, greenish eyes and twisted necks. It has a shelf-life of 20 years and it is a viral disease. Vaccinations include VH+HV-120, HIPRAVIAR and Newcastle lasota vaccine. Fowl typhoid is a bacterial disease caused by salmonella. Sympyoms include a pale yellow diarhoea. Its prevented through cleanliness and thorough house disinfection. Vaccines include: supermed TS1 + Alliseryl or Eryth-oxivit+Alliseryl. Coccidiosis is caused by a bacteria called protozoa. Symptoms are bloody diarhoea with fresh clots of blood. It is treated using AnticoX, Cocci D or ESB 3. Other diseases include infectious coryza, mites and scaly legs.
